A · Notable growth drivers
🚀 EUDI Wallet rollout (Dec 2026 / Dec 2027)
All EU states must issue EUDI Wallets by end-2026. Banks must accept them by Dec 2027. April 2026 EU regulation mandates ETSI TS 119 461 — creates immediate procurement demand for certified IDV providers.
🚀 eIDAS 2.0 & AML6 compliance wave
New implementing regs create a compliance floor. Companies already certified (like IDENTT) benefit disproportionately.
🚀 Neobank & fintech scaling
Digital-first financial services requiring 100% remote onboarding are driving the IDV demand surge across the BFSI vertical.
🚀 Deepfake & AI fraud acceleration
AI-generated identity fraud is driving rapid adoption of liveness detection and biometric verification even among mid-market buyers.
A · Notable headwinds
⚠️ National eID schemes displacing third-party IDV
Poland's mObywatel and other government ID schemes handle certain regulated flows natively.
⚠️ GDPR biometric data constraints
Strict EU restrictions on biometric data storage and retention limit deployment models.
⚠️ Fragmented EU eID architectures
27 different national implementations limit cross-border interoperability.
⚠️ Big Tech wallet connector risk
Google/Apple may capture the eIDAS 2.0 Wallet Connector market, marginalising third-party IDV providers.
